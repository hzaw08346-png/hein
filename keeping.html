Keeping & Feeding

Observing your fish during feeding time can tell you a lot. Are they all actively eating? Is anyone being outcompeted for food? Are they eating too greedily or spitting food out? These observations can help you adjust your feeding strategy. It's generally better to underfeed slightly than to overfeed. Uneaten food decomposes in the tank, releasing harmful ammonia, nitrites, and nitrates, which can stress your fish and lead to algae blooms. A good rule of thumb is to feed only what your fish can consume in a few minutes. If there's still food lingering after that time, you're likely feeding too much.
